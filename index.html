How to interview effectively...

Preparation time: Varies, but expect multiple days of research and planning
Professional rating: 100% Professional

Follow this quick and easy guide and you will be able to interview everyone like a pro!

  • Things You'll Need

  • A Subject
  • Notepad
  • Camera
  • Audio Recorder

  1. Find your subject and contact them to schedule an interview.

    Ideally, you will need to schedule the interview in advance so you have plenty of time to prepare. You should also take this time to research basic information about them: career, personal info, family life, achievements etc.

  2. Prepare an interview script with open-ended questions.

    The majority of your questions need to elicit a detailed response from our subject. Make your questions difficult to be answered with a simple "yes" or "no".

    Organize your notes in a way that they flow from one question into another. Almost like a script.

  3. It's time to interview!

    First, ask if you are allowed to record it to make sure you get everything right. During the interview, it's important to Be engaging, but don’t be afraid to stay silent to goad more answers out of them. Refer to the video below for extra tactics.

  4. Post-Interview Decisions.

    After the interview, ask around for friends and acquaintances of the person to get more background. If new information surfaces or if certain information was unclear, ask for a follow up interview.

  5. Writing and Publishing.

    This is the most important part. As a journalist, you must make sure that whatever you publish is clear and accurate. Use direct quotes or meaningful paraphrases. Edit your report with a peer and fact-check before publishing.
